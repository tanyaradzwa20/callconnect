5 Metrics That Actually Matter for Call Centres

April 28, 2023 7 min read 8 Comments

In the call centre industry, it's easy to get lost in a sea of data. While traditional metrics like Average Handle Time (AHT) have been industry standards for decades, forward-thinking call centres are shifting their focus to metrics that truly drive customer satisfaction and business growth. Here are the five metrics that actually matter for modern call centres.

1

First Contact Resolution (FCR)

Why it matters: FCR measures the percentage of customer issues resolved during the first interaction. This metric directly impacts customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

Key Benefits:

  • Reduced operational costs: Fewer repeat calls mean lower handling costs
  • Higher customer satisfaction: Customers appreciate getting their issues resolved quickly
  • Improved agent morale: Agents feel more accomplished when they solve problems effectively

How to Improve FCR:

  • Invest in comprehensive agent training
  • Implement robust knowledge management systems
  • Empower agents with decision-making authority
  • Use call monitoring to identify resolution barriers
2

Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT)

Why it matters: CSAT measures how satisfied customers are with your service. It's the most direct indicator of service quality.

Best Practices for CSAT Measurement:

  • Survey customers immediately after interactions
  • Keep surveys short and focused
  • Use consistent measurement scales
  • Act on the feedback received

Zimbabwe-Specific Considerations:

In the local market, we've found that personalized follow-ups and understanding cultural nuances significantly impact CSAT scores. Customers appreciate when agents demonstrate local market knowledge and cultural sensitivity.

3

Net Promoter Score (NPS)

Why it matters: NPS measures customer loyalty and the likelihood of recommendations. It's a powerful predictor of business growth.

Understanding NPS Categories:

  • Promoters (9-10): Loyal enthusiasts who will keep buying and refer others
  • Passives (7-8): Satisfied but unenthusiastic customers vulnerable to competitive offerings
  • Detractors (0-6): Unhappy customers who can damage your brand through negative word-of-mouth
4

Customer Effort Score (CES)

Why it matters: CES measures how easy it is for customers to get their issues resolved. Low-effort experiences drive loyalty.

Reducing Customer Effort:

  • Streamline call routing processes
  • Implement omnichannel support options
  • Provide self-service options for common issues
  • Train agents to anticipate customer needs
5

Employee Engagement Score

Why it matters: Engaged employees provide better customer service. This metric is often overlooked but crucial for long-term success.

Measuring Employee Engagement:

  • Regular employee satisfaction surveys
  • Turnover and absenteeism rates
  • Participation in training and development programs
  • Internal promotion rates

Call Connect's Approach to Employee Engagement:

We've implemented several initiatives that have significantly improved our employee engagement scores:

  • Career development programs with clear advancement paths
  • Regular recognition and reward systems
  • Continuous training on both technical and soft skills
  • Work-life balance initiatives tailored to the Zimbabwean context

Implementing a Balanced Scorecard Approach

Rather than focusing on individual metrics in isolation, successful call centres use a balanced scorecard approach that considers:

Customer Perspective:

  • CSAT and NPS scores
  • Customer retention rates
  • Service level agreement compliance

Internal Process Perspective:

  • First Contact Resolution
  • Average Handle Time (in context)
  • Quality assurance scores

Learning and Growth Perspective:

  • Employee engagement scores
  • Training effectiveness
  • Innovation implementation

Conclusion: Beyond the Numbers

While metrics provide valuable insights, they should inform rather than dictate your call centre strategy. The most successful operations use data as a starting point for continuous improvement, not as an end goal. By focusing on these five key metrics, call centres can drive meaningful improvements in both customer satisfaction and business performance.

At Call Connect, we've seen firsthand how shifting from traditional metric-focused management to a balanced, customer-centric approach has transformed our operations and delivered superior results for our clients across Zimbabwe.
